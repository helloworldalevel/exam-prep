Question 1 — Local Government Agency & Networks

14 marks

A local government agency uses IT in its operations. Employees work from the office, from home, or on the move.

1(a)(i) 1 mark Mcq
Employees store and access files using a local area network (LAN). Identify the type of storage used. A HDMI · B NAS · C RAID · D SSID
📘Context

The scenario: employees need shared storage that all LAN users can access from their own devices. You pick one of four acronyms that fit "type of storage accessed over a LAN".

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
NAS
Network-Attached Storage — a dedicated file-storage device plugged into the LAN, accessible by all users on the network.
HDMI
High-Definition Multimedia Interface — a video/audio cable/port, nothing to do with storage.
RAID
Redundant Array of Inexpensive Disks — multiple physical disks combined into one logical disk for redundancy/speed. It is a disk layout, not network storage.
SSID
Service Set Identifier — the human-readable name of a Wi-Fi network (e.g. "HomeRouter-5G"). Not storage.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
B (NAS)
Marks: NAS (1 mark)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • For MCQs, cross out the obviously wrong answers first — here, HDMI and SSID have nothing to do with storage, so you are between NAS and RAID.
  • Key distinction: NAS = network-accessible, RAID = multiple disks behaving as one. The question specifically says "using a LAN" which points to NAS.
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not confuse NAS with RAID — a NAS device often uses RAID internally, but they are different concepts. The question asks about the type of storage accessed over the LAN, which is NAS.
1(a)(ii) 2 marks Short
State two other ways employees can work together to collaborate in an online environment (other than file storage in the cloud).
📘Context

You already know they store files in the cloud — name two different collaboration methods besides that. Each is worth 1 mark.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Collaboration tools
Software or services that let multiple people contribute to the same work, either at the same time or asynchronously.
Video conferencing
Live video meetings (e.g. Zoom, Teams, Google Meet).
VOIP
Voice Over Internet Protocol — making phone calls using the internet rather than the traditional telephone network.
Instant messaging / chat
Real-time text exchange apps (e.g. Slack, Teams chat, WhatsApp).
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Any two of these, one mark each:
• Work on the same document collaboratively / at the same time
• Use video conferencing to hold meetings
• Use VOIP to make (free) telephone calls
• Use email / shared storage to exchange or share files or data or resources
• Use instant messaging / chat applications to exchange messages
Marks: Any two from the list above (1 mark each)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Give two distinctly different methods, not two instances of the same idea (e.g. "Zoom and Teams" is really one method — video conferencing — not two).
  • Naming a specific app (e.g. "Google Docs") is fine — examiners map it to its category (shared document editing).
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not accept: references to professional online communities. The question specifies collaboration tools, not community forums.
  • Do not write the storage method again (cloud file storage) — the stem says "other ways".
1(b) 2 marks Short
Give one positive and one negative impact of employees using online communities during work time.
📘Context

Two answers, one per line. Positive goes on the "Positive" line, negative on the "Negative" line. 1 mark each.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Online professional community
A forum/platform where professionals share knowledge and advice in their industry (e.g. Stack Overflow, LinkedIn groups, specialist Slack channels).
✅Mark Scheme Answer
POSITIVE (any one):
• Employees could get help with problems from other community members
• Employees could find new or better resources to use in their own work
• Employees could keep up to date on new products, legislation or news
• Employees positively promote the image of the agency, industry or profession

NEGATIVE (any one):
• Employees waste time / are unproductive / are distracted
• Employees may find other employment opportunities
• Employees could give away or reveal company-sensitive information
Marks: Positive (1 mark) + Negative (1 mark)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Write each point clearly on the correct line. Positive on "Positive", negative on "Negative".
  • One sentence each is enough — no need for long explanations.
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not award both marks for opposites. E.g. "Positive: share knowledge / Negative: share confidential info" is really the same point flipped — examiners give you 1 mark maximum.
  • If you put a negative on the positive line (or two positives), you get 1 mark maximum — read the line label before writing.
1(c)(i) 2 marks Linked
Explain one reason people may be concerned that the agency collects and stores data about them.
📘Context

Classic 2-mark "linked explanation" question. You need a reason (effect) AND a because-clause (reason) that supports it. Both halves must link together logically.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Linked explanation
A two-part answer where both halves are connected. Use "because" or "so that" to join them. The second mark is only awarded if the expansion associates with the statement.
Data protection
Laws (like GDPR/UK Data Protection Act) that govern how organisations must collect, store, and use personal data.
Personal profiling
Building a detailed picture of an individual from multiple data points, used for automated decision-making or targeted advertising.
📝Step-by-Step Walkthrough
  1. Pick one specific concern — don't try to cover everything.
  2. State the concern (effect) — what could go wrong.
  3. Write "because" and give the reason — why it could go wrong.
  4. Re-read to check both halves actually connect.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Award up to two marks for a LINKED explanation such as:
• The data may be vulnerable to malicious behaviours / unauthorised access / hacking (1) because it is not stored securely (1)
• The data could be shared with / sold to third parties (1) because the agency does not adhere to data-protection laws (1)
• The data could be used to automate decision making (1) because the agency can create personal profiles (1)
Marks: Statement (1 mark) + linked reason (1 mark). Both must associate.
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Structure your answer as: [effect] because [reason].
  • If you run out of time, writing at least one half earns 1 mark — don't leave it blank.
  • Use exam vocabulary: "vulnerable to", "hacked", "data-protection laws", "third parties".
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not accept consent. Saying "because people didn't consent" is explicitly not awarded.
  • Circular reasoning costs marks — e.g. "companies misuse data because they might misuse it" is saying the same thing twice.
1(c)(ii) 2 marks Linked
Describe what is meant by the term "inclusion" in the context of information technology systems.
📘Context

Inclusion in IT means making sure everyone can use systems regardless of personal circumstances. This is a "describe" question so you need detail — a one-word definition won't get 2 marks.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Inclusion
Designing and providing IT systems so that people from all backgrounds, ages, abilities, and circumstances can access and use them equally.
Accessibility
Specific features (screen readers, magnifiers, captions, alternative inputs) that remove barriers for users with disabilities.
Digital divide
The gap between people who have access to modern IT and those who do not (economic, geographic, age-related).
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Award up to two marks for a linked description such as:
• Providing everyone with equal access to devices / data / resources (1) regardless of personal circumstances — economic / race / age / gender / disability / religion / sexual orientation / cultural beliefs (1)
• Providing special facilities for accessing IT to people (1) with personal circumstances — economic / race / age / gender / disability / religion / sexual orientation / cultural beliefs (1)
Marks: General statement about access (1) + reference to the kinds of circumstances (1)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Name at least one specific type of circumstance (disability, age, economic background) to show you understand what inclusion covers.
  • Think of real examples: screen readers for blind users, subtitles for deaf users, large text for elderly users, free Wi-Fi for low-income users.
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Just writing "inclusion means everyone can use it" is too vague — you need the "regardless of X" or "special facilities for Y" detail to earn the second mark.
1(d)(i) 4 marks Table
Complete the table to identify the network metric described in the first column.
📘Context

Four short-answer cells, 1 mark each. You must memorise these four network metrics because they come up year after year.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Error rate
A measure of the number of bits transferred incorrectly (or proportion of bits received in error). Measured in BER (Bit Error Rate) or as a percentage.
Latency
The time delay between a data packet leaving the source and arriving at the destination. Measured in milliseconds (ms). Low latency = fast response.
Bandwidth
The maximum capacity of a communications channel — how much data it can carry per second. Measured in bits per second (bps, Mbps, Gbps).
Jitter
The variation in time between packets arriving at the destination. High jitter causes choppy video/audio calls even if bandwidth is fine.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Row 1 — "number of bits transferred incorrectly": Error rate (1)
Row 2 — "time delay between packet leaving source and arriving at destination": Latency (1)
Row 3 — "capacity of a communications channel": Bandwidth (1)
Row 4 — "variation in times between packets arriving": Jitter (1)
Marks: 1 mark for each correct cell
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Mnemonic: "Error, Latency, Bandwidth, Jitter" — in that order matches rows about: correctness, delay, capacity, variation.
  • Bandwidth = capacity (maximum). Latency = time to travel (delay). These two are often confused.
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Don't write "speed" for bandwidth — bandwidth is capacity, not speed. (A 1 Gbps connection with 500ms latency is "fast" in capacity but "slow" in response.)
  • Don't write "throughput" for bandwidth — throughput is actual data transferred, bandwidth is the maximum possible.
1(d)(ii) 1 mark Mcq
Identify which one of these is a feature of the world wide web. A E-mail · B Routers · C TCP/IP · D URLs
📘Context

A classic "Internet vs WWW" question. The World Wide Web is the hypertext system (websites, links, browsers) that runs on top of the Internet. The Internet is the underlying network.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Internet
The global network of networks — the underlying infrastructure (cables, routers, protocols).
World Wide Web (WWW)
A system of interlinked documents accessed via browsers using URLs, HTML, and HTTP. Built on top of the Internet.
URL
Uniform Resource Locator — the web address (e.g. https://example.com/page) that identifies a resource on the WWW.
TCP/IP
Transmission Control Protocol / Internet Protocol — the core protocol suite of the Internet (not specifically the WWW).
✅Mark Scheme Answer
D (URLs)
Marks: URLs (1 mark)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Memorise the distinction: email runs on SMTP/IMAP (Internet), routers are hardware (Internet infrastructure), TCP/IP is a transport protocol (Internet), URLs are specifically a WWW concept.
  • If it needs a browser, it's probably WWW. If it doesn't, it's probably just "Internet".
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Common trap: students pick "E-mail" because they associate it with the web. Email predates the WWW and uses its own protocols — it is an Internet service, not a WWW feature.
  • TCP/IP is the most tempting wrong answer if you don't know the distinction — it's a protocol, not a feature of the WWW specifically.

Question 2 — Networks, Encryption & Connectivity

16 marks

Organisations connect devices to networks. Networks are protected by a variety of security measures. The biggest section (2c) is worth 10 marks.

2(a) 2 marks Table
Complete the table to identify the encryption technique that matches each description: (row 1) sender and receiver use a shared key to encrypt and decrypt a message; (row 2) sender uses a public key to encrypt, receiver uses a private key to decrypt.
📘Context

Two rows, 1 mark each. The two techniques are symmetric and asymmetric — the question is which one goes in which row.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Symmetric encryption
Both sender and receiver use the same secret key to encrypt and decrypt. Fast but the key must be shared securely beforehand.
Asymmetric encryption
Uses a pair of keys — a public key (shared with anyone, used to encrypt) and a private key (kept secret, used to decrypt). Slower but solves the key-distribution problem.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Row 1 (shared key): Symmetric (1)
Row 2 (public key to encrypt, private key to decrypt): Asymmetric (1)
Marks: 1 mark for each correct cell
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Memory trick: Symmetric = Same key. Both start with S.
  • Asymmetric = Across two keys. A for "two-sided".
  • Accept "symmetrical" and "asymmetrical" — examiners allow the -al ending.
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not accept certificate-based in either cell.
  • Do not accept "private" or "shared" as synonyms for symmetric.
  • Do not accept "public" alone for asymmetric.
  • Classic mistake: swapping the two. Always double-check by re-reading the row descriptions against your answers.
2(b) 2 marks Linked
Some anti-malware software uses a database of known malware signatures to identify malicious software. Describe one other way anti-malware software could identify malicious software.
📘Context

The question rules out signature-based detection. You need to describe another detection method — and because it is worth 2 marks, it must be a linked description (method + how it works).

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Signature-based detection
Compares files to a database of known malware "fingerprints". Limitation: cannot catch brand-new malware.
Heuristic analysis
Examines the structure or behaviour of a program against a set of rules/characteristics to predict if it is malicious — works on unknown malware.
Behavioural monitoring
Watches what a program does while it runs, flagging suspicious activity (e.g. encrypting lots of files, modifying system settings).
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Award up to two marks for a linked description such as:
• Heuristics / set of rules / set of characteristics / descriptions of behaviours (1) are applied to executing software / applications (1)
• Heuristics / set of rules / set of characteristics (1) are used to identify malicious behaviours (1)
• Executing programs / applications are monitored (1) to identify unusual or harmful behaviours (1)
Marks: Detection method (1) + what it looks for / applies to (1)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Accept "running", "processing", "monitoring" as synonyms for "executing".
  • Give examples of malicious activity (e.g. "encrypting files without permission") — these count as equivalent to "behaviours".
  • Structure: [method] is used [what it does/looks for].
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not accept "sandbox" as a detection method — sandboxing is isolation, not detection.
  • Don't just write the name of a technique ("heuristics") — you need to say what it does to earn the second mark.
2(c) 10 marks Diagram
Complete the diagram by adding: a single line between devices that communicate with each other, and a label on the line stating the network communication protocol used. (Up to 10 marks.)
📘Context

The biggest and most valuable single question on the paper. You have to draw lines between the devices shown and label each line with the correct protocol. Each correct labelled line = 1 mark, up to 10. Thirteen possible connections are listed in the mark scheme.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Ethernet (802.3)
Wired LAN protocol — cables between servers, switches, routers, WAPs, printers.
Wi-Fi (802.11)
Wireless LAN protocol — connects laptops, phones, smart hubs to a wireless access point.
Bluetooth
Short-range wireless for peripherals (mouse, headphones, speakers). Also used for PAN tethering.
ZigBee
Low-power wireless for smart-home devices (bulbs, locks, thermostats). Often goes through a smart hub.
Cellular (GSM / 3G / 4G / 5G)
Mobile phone network — between a phone and a phone mast.
Infrared (IrDA)
Line-of-sight wireless for TV remotes, projector remotes, window-blind remotes.
USB
Wired peripheral connection (printers, keyboards, some mice).
HDMI
Wired video/audio connection (laptop to projector, laptop to monitor).
📝Step-by-Step Walkthrough
  1. Read the scenario: wireless is available in the meeting room but NOT in the break room. The printer does not support wireless.
  2. Draw the SERVER ROOM connections first: Servers ↔ WAP with Ethernet.
  3. BREAK ROOM: Phone ↔ Phone mast with Cellular (5G/4G). Smart bulb ↔ Smart hub with ZigBee. Note: phone cannot use Wi-Fi here because the room has no wireless.
  4. MEETING ROOM: Laptop ↔ WAP with Wi-Fi. Laptop ↔ Mouse with Bluetooth. Laptop ↔ Headphones with Bluetooth. Printer ↔ Laptop (or Printer ↔ server) with USB (or Ethernet) — printer has no wireless.
  5. PROJECTOR: Laptop ↔ Projector with HDMI. Projector ↔ WAP with Wi-Fi (optional smart projector).
  6. REMOTE CONTROLS (small hand-held keypads in the diagram): Remote ↔ Projector with Infrared. Remote ↔ Window blinds with Infrared.
  7. SMART HUB: Smart hub ↔ Smart lock with ZigBee. Smart hub ↔ WAP with Wi-Fi (so it can reach the internet).
  8. Count your connections — aim for 10 to maximise marks.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
All 13 accepted connections (pick the best 10):
1. Servers ↔ WAP with Ethernet / 802.3 (1)
2. Printer ↔ server room with Ethernet, OR Printer ↔ Laptop with USB (1)
3. Smart lock ↔ Smart hub with ZigBee (1)
4. Smart bulb ↔ Smart hub with ZigBee (1)
5. Phone ↔ Phone mast with Cellular / GSM / 3G/4G/5G (1)
6. Remote control ↔ Projector with Infrared / IrDA (1)
7. Remote control ↔ Window blinds with Infrared / IrDA (1)
8. Laptop ↔ Mouse with Bluetooth or USB (1)
9. Laptop ↔ Headphones with Bluetooth or USB (1)
10. Laptop ↔ WAP with Wi-Fi / 802.11 (1)
11. Smart hub ↔ WAP with Wi-Fi / 802.11 (1)
12. Laptop ↔ Projector with HDMI (1)
13. Projector ↔ WAP with Wi-Fi / 802.11 (1)
Marks: 1 mark per correct labelled connection, maximum 10
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • CRITICAL: Every line MUST have a written protocol label. An unlabelled line = 0 marks, even if the connection is correct.
  • If you find it hard to draw neatly, use a table format: From | To | Protocol. Three columns. This forces you to write a protocol for every row.
  • Allowed abbreviations: 802.11 = Wi-Fi, 802.3 = Ethernet, GSM/3G/4G/5G = Cellular, IrDA = Infrared. Mark scheme accepts both names.
  • Spelling: "Wi-Fi", "WiFi", "wifi", "Wifi" all accepted — examiners allow spelling and capitalisation flexibility.
  • Only 10 marks maximum — so 10 correct lines is a full score. Don't waste time on the 13th.
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not award marks for more than one line per pair of devices. Connecting Smart hub ↔ Smart bulb twice (once on the diagram, once in a table) doesn't give 2 marks — it gives 1.
  • Do not accept Wi-Fi from the phone — the question states no wireless in the break room. The phone must use cellular.
  • "Cable" alone is not a protocol — you must name the specific protocol (Ethernet, USB, HDMI).
  • Lines without labels are worth zero. Every. Single. Line. Must. Have. A. Word.
2(d) 2 marks Linked
An accountant has a smartphone and laptop and is sitting in a park. She needs to store her meeting notes (on her laptop) in the cloud. Describe how she can use her devices to store the notes in the cloud.
📘Context

She is outside — no Wi-Fi available. Her laptop doesn't have mobile internet. Only her phone has internet (via cellular). The answer is tethering: use the phone's mobile data to give the laptop internet access.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Tethering
Using a smartphone's mobile data to provide internet access to another device (laptop, tablet).
Mobile hotspot
A phone acting as a Wi-Fi access point — other devices connect to it via Wi-Fi.
Ad hoc wireless
A temporary wireless network created on-the-fly between two devices.
Personal Area Network (PAN)
A short-range network between a user's own devices — e.g. phone and laptop connected via Bluetooth tethering.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Award up to two marks for a linked description such as:
• Tethering / creation of an ad hoc wireless access point / mobile hotspot / PAN (via Bluetooth) (1) AND use the phone's cellular / mobile / 3G/4G network to access the internet / the cloud (1)
Marks: Tethering method (1) + cellular/mobile network (1). Both halves required.
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Two halves: (a) how the phone shares with the laptop (hotspot/tethering), (b) what the phone uses to reach the internet (cellular/mobile data).
  • Accept "broadband" as a synonym for cellular in this context.
  • Write it as a single linked sentence: "She tethers her phone as a mobile hotspot, using its 4G/cellular connection to give the laptop internet access to reach the cloud."
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Saying "connect to Wi-Fi" alone doesn't work — there is no Wi-Fi in the park. You must explain that the phone creates the hotspot using its cellular data.
  • Saying "use ethernet" is wrong — ethernet is wired; no cables in the park.
  • Only one half of the linked description earns 1 mark. You need both to earn 2.

Question 3 — Databases, SQL, Smart City & Software

18 marks

Bookstores use networks and software applications. This question covers database keys, data types, SQL queries, smart cities, and a 6-mark essay on open source vs proprietary software.

3(a)(i) 4 marks Table
Complete the table to give the field names of the keys described. Tables: tbl_employee, tbl_store, tbl_shift.
📘Context

You're given three partial tables. You need to identify: (1) tbl_employee primary key; (2) tbl_store primary key; (3) tbl_shift foreign key; (4) tbl_shift composite key.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Primary key
A field (or combination of fields) that uniquely identifies every row in a table. Usually an ID/number.
Foreign key
A field in one table that refers to the primary key of another table — used to link tables together.
Composite key
A primary key made up of multiple fields combined, because no single field is unique enough on its own.
📝Step-by-Step Walkthrough
  1. tbl_employee: each row is one employee. The unique ID is empNum.
  2. tbl_store: each row is one store. The unique ID is storeNum.
  3. tbl_shift foreign key: look for fields that match primary keys of other tables. "store" matches tbl_store.storeNum. "employee" matches tbl_employee.empNum. Either is accepted (but not both together).
  4. tbl_shift composite key: you need to find the minimum set of fields that makes every row unique. Look at sample data:   • Row 2: store=73, date=19-05-2023, start=11:00, employee=594998   • Row 4: store=73, date=19-05-2023, start=11:00, employee=838485   Store+date+start alone is NOT unique (these two rows have the same values!). You MUST add employee to disambiguate.
  5. So composite key = employee, date, start. "end" is not needed (it's a consequence of the shift, not part of its identity).
✅Mark Scheme Answer
tbl_employee Primary key: empNum (1)
tbl_store Primary key: storeNum (1)
tbl_shift Foreign key: store OR employee (1) — either answer, but not both
tbl_shift Composite key: employee, date, start (1) — all three required, order and punctuation ignored
Marks: 1 mark per correct cell (4 cells total)
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Primary keys are usually named things like "ID", "Num", "Number" — look for fields ending in those.
  • For composite keys, the test is: "what is the minimum combination that makes each row unique?" Try each combination against the sample data.
  • Foreign key row 3: either "store" or "employee" is correct — you don't need both (and writing both still earns only 1 mark).
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do NOT include "end" in the composite key — it is explicitly disallowed by the mark scheme.
  • Don't write "both" for foreign key — pick one.
  • A composite key of just "store, date, start" is wrong — the sample data proves it's not unique.
3(a)(ii) 2 marks Linked
The empNum field must be 6 digits long. One possible value is 062833. Explain one reason the empNum field cannot be assigned a data type of integer.
📘Context

The example value 062833 is your clue — it has a leading zero. Integers cannot store leading zeros because 062833 and 62833 are numerically the same number (sixty-two thousand eight hundred and thirty-three). If stored as an integer, the "0" would be dropped and the value would become 62833 — only 5 digits, breaking the 6-digit rule.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
Integer
A whole-number data type. Cannot store leading zeros, decimal points, or characters other than digits.
String / text
A data type that stores any sequence of characters — numbers, letters, symbols. Preserves leading zeros.
Fixed-length field
A field that must always contain exactly N characters — typical for IDs, postcodes, account numbers.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Award up to two marks for a linked explanation such as:
• The 0 at the start of the employee number would be dropped (1) because numbers / integers cannot have leading 0s / values with leading 0s must be stored as strings or characters (1)
Marks: Effect (1) + reason (1). Both halves must associate.
💡Hints & Exam Technique
  • Structure: [what happens] because [why it happens].
  • Use exact wording: "leading zero is dropped" / "integers cannot store leading zeros" / "must be stored as a string".
  • The question gives you 062833 as a worked example — refer to it directly: "The leading 0 in 062833 would be lost if stored as an integer".
⚠️Common Mistakes & "Do Not Accept"
  • Do not accept examples alone — you must explain why. Just writing "062833" as an answer isn't enough.
  • Writing only the reason (e.g. "integers can't have leading zeros") without the effect (what actually happens to the data) loses the first mark.
  • Writing only the effect (e.g. "it wouldn't work properly") without the reason loses the second mark.
3(b)(i) 2 marks Sql
Write an SQL statement that returns a set of unique titles available in one or both formats (books and DVDs).
📘Context

The database has two separate tables: tbl_book (with a title field) and tbl_dvd (also with a title field). You need to combine both lists into one set of titles with no duplicates — this is what UNION does.

🧠Key Terms & Concepts
UNION
SQL keyword that combines the results of two SELECT queries into a single result set, removing duplicates.
UNION ALL
Combines but keeps duplicates. Not what you want here since the question says "unique".
SELECT ... FROM
Basic SQL syntax to retrieve fields from a table.
📝Step-by-Step Walkthrough
  1. You cannot SELECT from two tables in one statement (unless joining, which is different). So you write TWO separate SELECT statements.
  2. First: SELECT title FROM tbl_book
  3. Then the keyword: UNION
  4. Then the second SELECT: SELECT title FROM tbl_dvd
  5. UNION automatically removes duplicates, so "The Plan" (in both tables) appears once.
✅Mark Scheme Answer
Award 1 mark for each of:
• SELECT <field> FROM <table> (1) — with a valid field/table combination
• Sight of UNION (1)

Model answer:
SELECT title FROM tbl_book
UNION
SELECT title FROM tbl_dvd;
Marks: SELECT/FROM clauses (1) + UNION keyword (1)
💡Hints & Exam Technique

Must have exactly ONE input and TWO outputs, with yes/no (or true/false) labels on the outputs.
Fully connected

  2. Accept first name, last name, email inputs (parallelogram I/O)
  3. Decision diamond: Cancel or Submit? → Cancel branch ends at End (oval). Submit branch continues.
  4. Validate form (process rectangle)
  5. Decision: Error on form? → Yes: Show error (parallelogram) → loop back to "accept inputs". No: continue.
  6. Decision: Email already registered? → Yes: Show error → loop back to "accept inputs". No: continue.
  7. Store new registration record in database (process rectangle)
  8. Generate unique registration number (process rectangle)
  9. Show registration number to customer (parallelogram I/O)

• D3: Fields validated, errors indicated, return to show form / accept inputs (1)
• D4: Check database for duplicate email, handled with error message, return to show form (1)
